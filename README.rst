Sphereical Galactic Environments: 2026 update
=============================================

DIRTY radiative transfer calculations for Witt & Gordon (2000, hereafter WG00)
spherical galactic environments (SGEs). The main goal is to expand the WG00 
calculations in wavelength to include the mid-infrared silicate features
and lower optical depths.  As part of this, the number of wavelength points
has been increased including finer sampling of the 2175 A bump and the
10 and 20 micron silicate features.

These calculations are done only the stellar photons, no dust emission is
included.  The focus is on predicting dust attenuation curves for different
SGEs (dusty, shell, cloudy), clumpy/homogenous local dust distributions, 
and different types of dust (MW, SMC, and mixtures of the two).
